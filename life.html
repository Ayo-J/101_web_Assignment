FOSS NSBM

FOSS of NSBM is a community established to spread the open source knowledge among students. Founded on 13th of December 2013, we have organized a number of events, including workshops. Our aim is to reduce the usage of pirated softwares and induce the usage of free and open source softwares.

In today's technologically advanced world, free and open-source operating systems like GNU, Linux, and BSD have gained immense popularity. These robust systems power millions of servers, desktops, smartphones (including Google Android), and various other devices. The utilization of free software licenses and open-source licenses extends beyond operating systems and is prevalent in numerous software packages as well. Recognizing the significance of open-source software and the potential it holds, the Starting Open Source Community at National School of Business Management aims to foster the development and encourage the skills of young students. This community is driven by the desire to contribute to society through the latest developments and novel concepts, ultimately transforming the lives of young individuals and making them feel like integral parts of the world's largest developing community. By providing a platform for students to explore the vast realm of open-source software, the community empowers them to harness their creativity, technical abilities, and problem-solving skills. Through collaborative projects and shared knowledge, students engage in real-world application of their learnings, gaining invaluable hands-on experience that prepares them for future endeavors. The community also serves as a hub of innovation, enabling students to experiment with cutting-edge technologies and stay updated with the latest trends in the open-source world. By actively participating in open-source projects, students not only expand their technical proficiency but also cultivate a deep sense of community involvement and global connectivity. Moreover, the Starting Open Source Community at National School of Business Management recognizes the transformative power of open-source software in bridging the digital divide and promoting inclusivity. By embracing open-source principles, students are equipped with the tools and resources necessary to create affordable and accessible solutions that can benefit individuals from diverse backgrounds. Through their contributions to open-source projects, students become part of a vibrant ecosystem where ideas are freely shared, collaboration is encouraged, and collective progress is prioritized. By fostering a culture of sharing, learning, and innovation, the community at National School of Business Management enables young minds to unlock their full potential and make meaningful contributions to the ever-evolving landscape of open-source software development. In essence, the Starting Open Source Community at National School of Business Management strives to empower and inspire young students, nurturing their skills and passion for open-source software. By embracing the spirit of collaboration and community engagement, this initiative aims to create a generation of individuals who not only benefit from open-source software but also actively contribute to its growth, making a lasting impact on society and the world at large.

IEEE NSBM

Institute of Electrical and Electronics Engineers at National School of Business Management.

IEEE NSBM (Institute of Electrical and Electronics Engineers at National School of Business Management) is a student branch of IEEE, the world's largest technical professional organization dedicated to advancing technology for the benefit of humanity. IEEE NSBM operates within the National School of Business Management, serving as a platform for students to explore and engage with various aspects of electrical engineering, electronics, computer science, and related fields. The primary objective of IEEE NSBM is to provide students with opportunities to enhance their knowledge, skills, and professional development in these technical domains. The student branch organizes a wide range of activities, including workshops, seminars, technical sessions, competitions, and hands-on projects, that cater to the diverse interests and needs of its members. Through these activities, IEEE NSBM aims to foster a vibrant learning environment where students can exchange ideas, collaborate on projects, and gain practical experience. By organizing technical events, the student branch promotes the exploration of emerging technologies, industry trends, and research advancements. These events often feature distinguished guest speakers, experts from academia and industry, who share their insights and expertise with the student community. Furthermore, IEEE NSBM encourages its members to actively participate in research and innovation. The student branch provides a supportive platform for students to showcase their projects, research findings, and technical expertise through conferences and symposiums. This fosters a culture of academic excellence and intellectual growth, nurturing future engineers and scientists. Beyond technical activities, IEEE NSBM also emphasizes the development of soft skills and leadership abilities. The student branch offers workshops and training sessions focused on communication, teamwork, project management, and professional ethics. These initiatives aim to equip students with a well-rounded skill set, enabling them to thrive not only in technical domains but also in their future careers. IEEE NSBM actively collaborates with industry partners, academic institutions, and other IEEE student branches, fostering a broader network of connections and opportunities for its members. Through such collaborations, students gain exposure to real-world challenges, industry best practices, and potential career pathways. Being part of IEEE NSBM provides students with access to a wealth of resources and benefits. Members have access to IEEE's extensive digital library, which offers a vast collection of research papers, technical publications, and educational materials. Additionally, students can participate in global IEEE competitions, conferences, and networking events, connecting with peers and professionals from around the world. Overall, IEEE NSBM serves as a dynamic platform for students at the National School of Business Management to immerse themselves in the world of technology, gain practical knowledge, and develop a strong foundation for their future careers. By fostering a community of passionate learners and professionals, the student branch contributes to the growth and advancement of engineering and technology, while empowering its members to make a positive impact in their respective fields..

CISS NSBM

The Circle of Information System Science at NSBM.

The Circle of Information System Science at NSBM is a student organization dedicated to promoting and exploring the field of Information Systems. It operates within NSBM, providing a platform for students interested in information systems to come together, learn, and engage in activities related to the discipline. The primary aim of the Circle of Information System Science is to foster a community of students passionate about Information Systems and to facilitate their growth and development in the field. The organization organizes a range of activities and events that aim to enhance members' knowledge, skills, and understanding of Information Systems. One of the key activities of the Circle of Information System Science is organizing workshops, seminars, and guest lectures by industry professionals and experts in the field of Information Systems. These events cover a variety of topics such as database management, system analysis and design, enterprise resource planning, business intelligence, and emerging trends in information technology. By bringing in industry expertise, the organization ensures that its members are exposed to current industry practices and real-world applications of Information Systems. Additionally, the Circle of Information System Science encourages its members to engage in hands-on learning experiences and practical projects. Members are provided with opportunities to work on real-world projects, develop information systems, and gain practical experience in areas such as software development, database management, and system implementation. These practical experiences help students bridge the gap between theoretical knowledge and practical application. Furthermore, the organization emphasizes the importance of networking and professional development. Members have the chance to interact with professionals from the industry, attend career fairs, and participate in industry visits. These activities allow students to build connections and gain insights into career opportunities in the field of Information Systems. Moreover, the Circle of Information System Science encourages research and innovation among its members. It provides a platform for students to present their research findings, engage in academic discussions, and contribute to the advancement of Information Systems knowledge. The organization may also facilitate collaborations with faculty members and industry partners to undertake research projects and explore emerging areas within the field. Being a part of the Circle of Information System Science at NSBM offers several benefits to its members. It provides a supportive community where students can share ideas, collaborate on projects, and enhance their understanding of Information Systems. Members gain access to resources such as research papers, academic journals, and industry reports, enabling them to stay updated with the latest developments in the field. Overall, the Circle of Information System Science at NSBM plays a significant role in nurturing the interest and skills of students in the field of Information Systems. By providing opportunities for learning, practical experience, networking, and research, the organization prepares its members for successful careers and enables them to contribute to the advancement of Information Systems science.

ISACA NSBM

.

In celebration of the ISACA Community Day 2022, the ISACA Chapter of NSBM Green University organized an IT security awareness programme targeting A/L and O/L students to make them aware of the cyber and social media attacks and mitigation techniques. The session was held on 5th October at Kottawa Ananda Maha Vidyalaya with the participation of 200+ students from A/L and O/L batches. The students were able to experience a real-time phishing attack in an isolated environment and gained awareness of mitigating such attacks. Senior Lecturer – Mr. Chamara Disanayake and Lecturer – Mr. Dilhara Batan Arachchige of the Department of Network and Security, Faculty of Computing at NSBM, facilitated the awareness session along with the students of NSBM ISACA chapter..
